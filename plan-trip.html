Tourist Visa Types

Saudi Arabia offers several visa options for tourists, making it easier than ever to visit the Kingdom:

üì± eVisa

Processing: Instant to 24 hours
Validity: 1 year, multiple entry
Stay: Up to 90 days per visit
Cost: 535 SAR (including insurance)

üö™ Visa on Arrival

Eligibility: Citizens of USA, UK, EU countries, and others
Processing: At airport
Requirements: Valid passport, return ticket

üïã Transit Visa

Purpose: Stopover tourism
Validity: 96 hours
Cost: Free for eligible airlines
Stay: Maximum 4 days

üé´ GCC Resident Visa

Eligibility: Residents of GCC countries
Processing: Online application
Requirements: Valid residence permit

How to Apply for eVisa

  1. Visit the official Saudi visa portal: visa.visitsaudi.com
  2. Create an account with valid email address
  3. Fill out the application form with personal details
  4. Upload required documents (passport copy, photo)
  5. Pay visa fee using credit/debit card
  6. Receive eVisa via email (usually within hours)
  7. Print visa or save digital copy for entry

Entry Requirements

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months from entry date
  • Passport photo meeting ICAO standards
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking)
  • Return or onward flight ticket
  • Travel insurance (included in eVisa fee)
  • Sufficient funds for stay (credit card acceptable)

Important Notes

  • Israeli passport holders and passports with Israeli stamps cannot enter Saudi Arabia
  • Visitors with criminal records may be denied entry
  • Some nationalities may require additional documentation
  • Always check latest requirements on official government websites
  • Umrah and Hajj visas are separate from tourist visas

Domestic Transportation

Domestic Flights

  • Saudia Airlines: National carrier with extensive network
  • Flynas: Low-cost carrier connecting major cities
  • Flyadeal: Budget airline for domestic routes
  • Cost: 150-600 SAR depending on route and timing
  • Booking: Online booking recommended for best prices

Trains

  • Haramain High-Speed Railway: Connects Makkah, Jeddah, and Madinah
  • Speed: Up to 300 km/h
  • Duration: Jeddah to Makkah: 30 minutes
  • Tickets: 50-250 SAR depending on class

Car Rental

  • International Companies: Hertz, Avis, Budget, Europcar
  • Local Companies: Theeb, Lumi, Yelo
  • Requirements: International driving license, credit card, age 21+
  • Cost: 100-300 SAR per day depending on vehicle
  • Note: Riyadh and Jeddah have heavy traffic during peak hours

Ride-Sharing

  • Uber: Available in all major cities
  • Careem: Regional competitor, widely used
  • Cost: Generally affordable, surge pricing during peak times
  • Payment: Cash or card accepted

Public Transportation

  • Riyadh Metro: Modern system with 6 lines (opened 2024)
  • Buses: Available in major cities but limited routes
  • Taxis: Metered taxis available, negotiate price beforehand if meter broken

‚ÑπÔ∏è Important Information

Cultural Guidelines

  • Dress Code: Dress modestly; shoulders and knees should be covered in public
  • Photography: Ask permission before photographing people, especially women
  • Ramadan: Respect fasting hours; eating in public during daylight restricted
  • Alcohol: Strictly prohibited throughout the Kingdom
  • Prayer Times: Shops may close briefly during prayer times (5 times daily)
  • Gender Separation: Some venues have family and single sections
  • Greetings: Handshakes common among same gender; wait for opposite gender to extend hand

Health and Safety

  • Medical Facilities: Excellent hospitals in major cities
  • Travel Insurance: Included with eVisa or purchase separately
  • Emergency Numbers: Police 999, Ambulance 997, Fire 998
  • Water: Tap water treated but bottled water recommended
  • Sun Protection: Essential due to strong sun year-round
  • Vaccinations: No special vaccines required for most visitors

Communication

  • Language: Arabic official; English widely spoken in tourist areas
  • SIM Cards: Available at airports and mobile shops (bring passport)
  • Providers: STC, Mobily, Zain offer tourist packages
  • Wi-Fi: Available in hotels, cafes, and many public spaces
  • Cost: Tourist SIM cards start from 50-100 SAR for 1-2 weeks

Currency and Payments

  • Currency: Saudi Riyal (SAR); 1 USD ‚âà 3.75 SAR (fixed rate)
  • Credit Cards: Widely accepted in cities; carry cash for small vendors
  • ATMs: Readily available; international cards accepted
  • Tipping: 10-15% in restaurants if service charge not included
  • Bargaining: Acceptable in traditional souks, not in malls
